## KEY FINDINGS & OBSERVATIONS

### Content Structure
- **Theme:** Why the Schlieffen Plan for quick victory failed
- **Time Period:** World War I (1914), specifically pre-Battle of the Marne through first months of war
- **Geographic Focus:** France, Belgium, Germany (Western Front)
- **Central Concept:** Plan ↔ Reality gap (theory vs. implementation)

### Educational Design
- **Task Progression:** Moves from fact-recall (Task 1-2) through sequencing/analysis (Tasks 3-6) to synthesis (Task 7)
- **Material Progression:** Strategic plan → Geographical reality → Soldier perspective → Battle outcome → Lasting consequence
- **Pedagogical Strategy:** Multimodal (text, maps, diary, photo) to engage different learning styles
- **Emotional Arc:** Hope (soldiers' belief in quick victory) → Disillusionment (reality of war)

### Technical Implementation
- **Type:** Client-side JavaScript Single-Page Application (no server-side rendering)
- **Tip System:** Progressive unlock (must see Tipp 1 before Tipp 2)
- **Feedback System:** Likely modal/overlay presentations of success/error messages
- **State Management:** Appears to persist task completion and progress locally
